The emotional hook

The Backstory Judges Will Remember

Most hackathon demos start with "We built a multi-agent system that..." — judges hear that fifty times. SentinelOps starts with a person under pressure.

The story: Amara Diallo is VP of Legal & Compliance at Meridian Ventures. A $1.8 million partnership contract just arrived — 74 pages. The board expects her signature in 48 hours. Her legal team is working through it, but 48 hours is not enough to cross-reference 10 sections across 74 pages, check every clause against company history, verify board resolutions, and stress-test the financial terms. Not with the rigor this deal demands. She's not careless. She's under-resourced against a deadline. This is the enterprise reality SentinelOps was built for. Not replacing lawyers. Giving them the kind of deep, multi-perspective review that normally takes a week — in minutes. Five agents review every clause from five angles simultaneously through Band, and deliver a quantified brief with a full audit trail she can hand to the board. What they find: $2,420,000 in hidden financial exposure. A board resolution violation. A vendor her company already rejected once. A 5-year lock-in buried across two sections 33 pages apart. Verdict: Do not sign as written. Here are the six things to renegotiate.

This is not a story about AI replacing humans. It's about AI giving a capable professional the depth she doesn't have time for. That framing matters — Track 3 is about careful decision-making, not automation. Judges in regulated/high-stakes tracks reward systems that augment human authority, not bypass it.
